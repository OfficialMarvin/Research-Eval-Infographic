Finding a Source

Begin your research with a clear focus and use the following steps to assess the credibility of your sources:

  • Determine the scope of your research to guide your source selection.
  • Use reliable databases and search engines to gather initial sources.
  • Read abstracts or summaries to gauge the relevance of the material.

Research Publisher

Analyze the publisher to ensure the source's reliability:

  • Check the publisher's reputation and review process for quality assurance.
  • Identify the publisher's mission and goals to detect any biases.

Research Author

The author's credibility is paramount in evaluating a source:

  • Investigate the author's academic background and expertise in the field.
  • Examine the author's publication history for consistency and quality.
  • Consider the author's reputation and recognition by peers.

Last Details

Pay attention to these final but critical aspects:

  • Verify the date of publication to ensure the source is up-to-date.
  • Look for citations and references as a sign of thorough research.
  • Assess the objectivity of the source, watching for biased language or a lack of evidence.
